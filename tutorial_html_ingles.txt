## English
### About

This tutorial has the purpose to help you fill out the spreadsheets. Each section in the spreadsheet is represented separately in this tutorial with examples and details of some required information that may be confusing when you are filling it out.
We tried to cover different examples however we recognize that there may be other structure types inserted in different contexts which differ from the examples given here If you have any questions, please contact us by e mail passagens.fauna@gmail.com.

#### Columns `structure_height`, `structure_width`, `structure_length` e `waterbody_width`

##### • Conventional structures
Measurements to be reported, as height (`structure_height` = **a**), width (`structure_width` = **b**) e length (`structure_length` = **c**, **c1**, **c2**) of the underpasses (Figure \@ref(fig:structuremedidas)). The values must be reported in *meters**.
It should be noted that the length may not be equal to the road width, since there are scaly structures (**c1**, skewed) in relation to the road axis or on roads with an embankment, where the structure extends beyond the width of the road and shoulder (**c2**).

```{r structuremedidas, echo=FALSE, fig.cap='Schematics for conventional structure showing the ways of obtaing the parameters of height (**a**), width(**b**) and length (**c1**, **c2**) of the underpasses.', fig.align='center', out.width='80%'}
knitr::include_graphics(c("Imagens/under_medidas.jpg"))
```

##### • Pontes
In case of bridges the measurements is different.
Measurements to be reported, as height (`structure_height` = **a**), width (sum) of the stream width under bridges (`structure_width` = **b**), extension (`structure_length` = **c**) and length of waterbody under the bridge (`waterbody_width` = **d**) of the underpasses (Figure \@ref(fig:structuremedidasagua)). The values must be reported in *meters**.

```{r structuremedidasagua, echo=FALSE, fig.cap="Schematics for bridges showing the ways of obtaining the of height (**a**), width (sum) of the stream width under bridges (**b**), length (**c**) and length of waterbody under the bridge (**d**).", fig.align='center', out.width='100%'}
knitr::include_graphics(c("Imagens/ponte_bd.jpg"))
```

#### Column `fence_overhang`

The most common type, without top flap (Figure \@ref(fig:cercasemaba)).

##### • Fence with folded overhang
Fence with an angled top flap, in rigid material, usually angled to outside the road (Figure \@ref(fig:cercaabarigida)).

```{r cercaabarigida, echo=FALSE, fig.cap='Fence with folded overhang.', fig.align='center', out.width='50%'}

##### • Fence with flexible overhang
Fence with flexible top flap in less rigid material, it has the purpose to gives in with the weight of the animal (Figure \@ref(fig:cercaflexivel)).
